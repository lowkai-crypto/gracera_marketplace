# User Personas

Understanding who uses Gracera shapes every design and AI decision on the platform.

---

## Supplier Side

### Persona S1 — The Export Manufacturer

**Name:** David Chen
**Company:** Mid-size electronics components factory in Shenzhen
**Role:** Export Sales Manager

**Context:**
- Produces PCB assemblies, connectors, and enclosures
- Currently relies on Alibaba, trade shows, and referrals
- Wants to expand into North American and European markets
- English is a second language; relies on sales team for English comms

**Goals:**
- Find buyers in target export markets without paying per-lead fees
- Establish credibility with certifications (ISO 9001, RoHS)
- Get RFQs that match their MOQ and production capacity

**Pain Points:**
- Inquiries from Alibaba are often low-quality tire-kickers
- Trade shows are expensive and results are unpredictable
- Doesn't know how to reach decision-makers at target companies

**What They Provide on Gracera:**
- Product catalog with specs, MOQ, lead times
- Factory certifications and audit reports
- Target markets and buyer types (OEM, distributor, reseller)
- Capacity and turnaround SLAs

---

### Persona S2 — The Domestic Service Vendor

**Name:** Maria Santos
**Company:** Commercial cleaning and facilities management, Texas
**Role:** Owner / Sales Lead

**Context:**
- 12-person company serving office buildings and retail chains
- Growth is entirely referral-based; no digital presence for B2B sales
- Looking to win contracts with regional retail chains or property managers

**Goals:**
- Get found by facilities managers at companies with 5+ locations
- Showcase references, insurance, and bonding credentials
- Receive qualified RFQs without paying a broker

**Pain Points:**
- No time to do outbound sales; business runs on her personally
- Existing lead-gen services send unqualified, price-shopping leads

**What They Provide on Gracera:**
- Service coverage area (ZIP codes / metro areas)
- Service types and specialty certifications
- Insurance and bonding information
- Reference clients (with permission)
- Target customer types: retail, commercial real estate, hospitality

---

### Persona S3 — The Independent Sales Rep

**Name:** James Park
**Company:** Independent rep, representing 3 Korean food ingredient brands
**Role:** B2B Sales Representative

**Context:**
- Represents brands that want North American distribution
- Does not own inventory; earns commission on deals closed
- Needs to find food distributors, restaurant chains, and grocery importers

**Goals:**
- Build a pipeline of buyer leads for each brand he represents
- Present multiple brands to a single buyer efficiently
- Track deal status across multiple relationships

**Pain Points:**
- Hard to differentiate himself from hundreds of other reps approaching the same buyers
- No easy way to show buyers his track record and represented brands

**What They Provide on Gracera:**
- Rep profile with track record and territory
- Brand cards for each represented supplier
- Target buyer types and deal sizes

## Buyer Side

### Persona B1 — The Procurement Manager

**Name:** Rachel Kim
**Company:** Mid-size US retail chain (50 stores)
**Role:** Director of Procurement

**Context:**
- Sources private-label goods, packaging, and store supplies
- Manages vendor relationships across 8 categories
- Under pressure to reduce COGS by 12% this year

**Goals:**
- Find qualified alternative suppliers to create negotiating leverage
- Shortlist 3–5 vetted vendors per category quickly
- Ensure vendors meet compliance requirements (labor, environmental)

**Pain Points:**
- Sourcing new vendors manually takes 3–6 weeks per category
- Trade show discovery is annual; too slow for urgent needs
- Hard to verify vendor claims without visiting factories

**What They Provide on Gracera:**
- Sourcing needs by category with volume, timeline, and geography
- Compliance requirements (certifications, audits required)
- Preferred deal structure (annual contract, PO-by-PO, etc.)

---

### Persona B2 — The Small Business Importer

**Name:** Tony Russo
**Company:** Sole-proprietor importing kitchenware from Asia, sells on Amazon and own DTC site
**Role:** Owner / Buyer

**Context:**
- Sources 4–6 product lines per year
- Currently uses Alibaba but has had quality and communication issues
- Annual import volume ~$150K USD

**Goals:**
- Find reliable manufacturers who communicate well and hit lead times
- Get samples quickly before committing to production
- Understand landed cost (product + shipping + duties)

**Pain Points:**
- Difficult to vet suppliers without factory visits
- Language and time-zone barriers slow negotiations
- Has been burned by late shipments and quality failures

**What They Provide on Gracera:**
- Product categories and volume ranges
- Sample request requirements
- Quality expectations (materials, inspection, packaging)
- Preferred shipping incoterms

---

### Persona B3 — The Reseller / Distributor

**Name:** Linda Nguyen
**Company:** Regional food & beverage distributor, Southeast US
**Role:** Category Buyer

**Context:**
- Distributes to 300+ independent grocery stores and restaurants
- Actively looking for emerging brands and unique imported products
- Prefers suppliers who can provide marketing materials and trade support

**Goals:**
- Discover new brands before competitors do
- Find suppliers who understand distributor margins and deal terms
- Get exclusive regional distribution rights where possible

**Pain Points:**
- Inbound brand pitches are high volume but low relevance
- Hard to filter for brands that are "distributor-ready" (compliant labels, palletized shipping, etc.)

**What They Provide on Gracera:**
- Distribution territory and channel (grocery, foodservice, specialty)
- Category focus and annual distribution volume
- Requirements: label compliance, insurance, trade terms
